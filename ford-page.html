Born out of an intense personal grudge and designed for a singular obsession: to defeat Ferrari at Le Mans. The GT40 is the ultimate icon of American willpower and engineering might. It didn't just compete on the world stage; it conquered it, sweeping the podium with a legendary 1-2-3 finish in 1966. Four consecutive 24-hour victories proved that this brutally fast, seven-liter masterpiece was unstoppable. Low-slung, powerful, and utterly dominant, it's not just a race car; it's the defining symbol of an era where America rewrote the racing rulebook.
